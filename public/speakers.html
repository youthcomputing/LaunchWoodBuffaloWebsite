Mark Scantlebury

Extreme Telematics Corp.

Craig Elias

Chiu School of Business

The most recent recipient of the World Federation of Colleges and Polytechnics bi- annual Gold Award of Excellence in Entrepreneurship, Craig Elias is an award-winning educator, entrepreneur, author, and a highly sought-after startup advisor.   After winning a $1,000,000 prize in a global billion-dollar idea pitch competition and spending time in Silicon Valley with his first venture, Craig returned home to help startups in and around Calgary.     Since then Craig has been a National Growth Advisor for Goldman Sachs’ and Babson College’s 10,000 Small Business Program in Boston, a startup mentor for the highly acclaimed Alchemist Accelerator in San Francisco, on an advisory committee for South by Southwest (SXSW) in Austin, and on the advisory board for Nudge.ai (an artificial intelligence company started by a founder of Eloqua, which was sold to Oracle for $957 Million US) in Toronto.   For the past six years, Craig has been the Entrepreneur-in-Residence at Bow Valley College in downtown Calgary where he gives away over $100,000 each year to help post-secondary students across the province of Alberta become first-time entrepreneurs.   He also runs Calgary’s five-day ‘Innovation Rodeo’ and three different student pitch competitions – VentureQuest, 150Startups, and INVENTURE$.

Jeniffer Kennet

Fuse Social

Jennifer Kennett is Programming Director at Fuse Social. With over 12 years of experience in Human Resources through various sectors: private business, industry and municipal government, Jennifer found herself leaning away from corporate life and seeking the opportunity to make a difference in our community. A pivotal moment in her career was when she was accepted into the Leadership Wood Buffalo cohort for 2017/2018 and, from there, work stopped being work and she stepped into a sector that she loves. Living in the urban Centre of Fort McMurray with her husband, Danny, and 2 daughters, Annabelle and Emilie, Jennifer has 4 generations of family residing in the region. Her roots run deep in Wood Buffalo and she strives to empower those that she meets to become greater versions of themselves and to build meaningful relationships with various stakeholders and community members. Being that these are Jennifer’s strengths, it only makes sense that she would be drawn to FuseSocial where she can help strengthen Social Profits to provide the greatest impact for our region.

Diana Noble

Snap'd Wood Buffalo

Diana has volunteered in a variety of ways throughout the community since first moving to Fort McMurray over a decade ago. She was a business advisor for the Junior Achievement program for several years, inspiring youth to become entrepreneurs. She lent her time as a board member for one term with the Canadian Mental Health Association, and is currently a Director on the board of the Fort McMurray Chamber of Commerce as well as the Fort McMurray Real Estate Board. She has facilitated workshops on entrepreneurship for the Justin Slade Youth Foundation’s “No Limits Youth Empowerment Day,” as well as conducted multiple marketing & branding workshops for the Fort McMurray Chamber of Commerce and RMWB Economic Development. She was nominated as a Woman of Inspiration for the Girls Inc. of Northern Alberta Women of Inspiration Series 2016/2017, and was recognized as a Top 50 Under 50 by Your McMurray Magazine in 2017 and a Top 40 Under 40 by The Connect in 2012. Her business snapd Wood Buffalo was nominated for Micro Business of the Year for the Chamber Awards in 2015, won the Fort McMurray Tourism Gold Star Award for Media in 2016, and was nominated for the Arts Council Wood Buffalo Excellence in Arts Awards under the category for Literary Arts in 2018.

Darshana Dave

Wood Buffalo Economic Development

Darshana Dave is an Economic Development Officer at Wood Buffalo Economic Development (WBEDC) and Program Manager of Wood Buffalo Regional Innovation Network (WBRIN). With over 10 years of experience in Entrepreneurship, Business Strategy, Marketing, and Operations through various sectors: Technology; Startup Incubator and Accelerators; Non-Profits; Social Entrepreneurship; Post Secondary Education and Healthcare, Darshana has developed the expertise to support Start-ups and Small Businesses at different stages of their growth. Darshana is an avid networker with growing connections and resources for supporting businesses in Canada and Internationally. She has a double Master’s degree in Science and Management along with BCIT’s Associate Certificate in Entrepreneurship. Darshana also manages the WBRIN group, an Alberta Innovates’ initiative, that includes representatives from Keyano College, Community Futures Wood Buffalo, Fort McMurray Chamber of Commerce, Northeastern Alberta Aboriginal Business Association (NAABA) and Wood Buffalo Economic Development Corporation (WBEDC). In her journey, she has supported more than 2000 Start-ups at different stages and is working towards developing programs at WBEDC for the inception of start-ups and growth of entrepreneurs in the Wood Buffalo region.
